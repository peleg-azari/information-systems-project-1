Customer Experience: Meeting Expectations

Introduction

The customer experience lives on a spectrum from flawless to infuriating.
You’ll always remember the tailor who happily completed a last-minute alteration for an important event.
At the same time, you’ll also remember the restaurant staff that unapologetically provided bad service and didn’t bother to remedy the situation.

Customers are starting to expect more of these positive, memorable experiences, and the pressure is mounting for
businesses to step up their CX game. According to the Zendesk Customer Experience Trends Report 2023 , 81 percent of business leaders see
customer experience and support as growing priorities over the next year, proving that organizations see this as a differentiator in the marketplace.

Importance to Companies

CX is important because every exchange between a customer and a business no matter how small has the power to build or damage the relationship.
These interactions can be something impactful, like a conversation between a consumer and a support agent, or something as simple as paying a bill or viewing a company ad on social media.

How customers feel about your brand is directly tied to customer retention, lifetime value, and brand loyalty. A good CX drives competitive advantage,
and companies that can deliver an outstanding experience stand out from the competition. According to our CX Trends Report, 60 percent of consumers have purchased from a brand solely based on the service they expect to receive

Data Systems for Analysis

Companies can utilize various data systems to analyze customer experience, including:

  • Customer Relationship Management (CRM) systems
  • Point-of-Sale (POS) systems
  • Customer feedback and survey platforms
  • Web analytics tools
  • Big data analytics platforms
  • Machine learning and Ai technologies

Case Use: Leveraging Data Analytics for Enhanced Retail Customer Experience with Onebeat

In today's retail environment, consumers expect to find their desired products readily available when they visit stores. Empty shelves not only disappoint but also drive consumers away. This is where Onebeat, leveraging Information Systems Engineering, steps in with its data-driven approach to inventory management. By harnessing real-time data using AI and machine learning, Onebeat ensures that the right product is in the right spot, predicting demand and optimizing inventory levels. Their algorithm considers various factors, including color, size, location, and sales history, to differentiate between head and tail products. By assigning different weights to each data element and prioritizing the latest data over older data, Onebeat makes short-term predictions, ensuring that shelves are stocked with the products customers want, when they want them. This meticulous approach leads to improved customer satisfaction and increased revenue.
